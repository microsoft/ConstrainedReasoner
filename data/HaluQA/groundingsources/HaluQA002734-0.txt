Bruno Olbrycht (nom de guerre: Olza; 6 October 1895 – 23 March 1951) was a soldier of the Austro-Hungarian Army and officer (later general) of the Polish Army both in the Second Polish Republic and postwar Poland. Born on 6 October 1895 in Sanok, Austrian Galicia, Olbrycht fought in Polish Legions in World War I, Polish–Ukrainian War, Polish–Soviet War and the Invasion of Poland.Galicia (Ukrainian and Rusyn: Галичина, "Halychyna"; Polish: "Galicja" ; Czech and Slovak: "Halič" ; German: "Galizien" ; Hungarian: "Galícia/Kaliz/Gácsország/Halics" ; Romanian: "Galiția/Halici" ; Russian: Галиция , "Galitsiya"; Yiddish: גאַליציע‎ , "Galitsye") is a historical and geographic region in Central-Eastern Europe, once a small Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria that straddled the modern-day border between Poland and Ukraine.
