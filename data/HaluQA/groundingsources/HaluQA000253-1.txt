Henry Bacon (November 28, 1866 – February 16, 1924) was an American Beaux-Arts architect who is best remembered for the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C. (built 1915–22), which was his final project.The Lincoln Memorial is an American national monument built to honor the 16th President of the United States, Abraham Lincoln. Dedicated in 1922, it is one of several monuments built to honor an American president.
