Somewhere near Lake Baikal on the Siberian steppes, archeologists were opening 7,000-year-old graves. The bodies had been carefully interred. One was buried with a long, carved spoon. Another had been honoured with a necklace of elk teeth. “They look like people being buried — except they're dogs,” said Robert Losey, a University of Alberta archeologist.
