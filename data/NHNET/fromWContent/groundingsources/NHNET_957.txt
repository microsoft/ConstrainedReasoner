Italy's historic city of Venice was left partly under water after a flood defense system did not work on time due to a mistaken weather forecast, local media reported Wednesday. A system of retractable flood barriers that is designed to work when high tides reach 1.3 meters (around 4.3 feet), failed to activate because Tuesday's tide was only predicted to rise to 1.2 meters.
