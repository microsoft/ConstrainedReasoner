Scientists have unearthed in Argentina's Patagonian wilderness fossils of what may be the oldest-known member of the dinosaur group that includes the largest land animals in Earth's history. Researchers said on Monday the fossils represent a titanosaur species named Ninjatitan zapatai that lived 140 million years ago during the Cretaceous Period.
