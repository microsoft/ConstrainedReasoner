Yet another sad news shakes the Hindi and the Marathi film industry today Nishikant Kamat, who was known for films like Drishyam and Madaari, passed away today in Hyderabad. The director had been hospitalised for the past couple of days in Hyderabad as he had been diagnosed with chronic liver disease and other secondary infections.
The director had suffered from cirrhosis of the liver in the past. That had relapsed thus compounding matters. Earlier the hospital had released a statement saying, “Mr Nishikant Kamat (50 years, male) was brought to AIG hospitals, Gachibowli, Hyderabad on 31st July with jaundice and abdominal distention. He has been diagnosed with Chronic Liver Disease and other secondary infections. He is in the Intensive Care Unit for continuous monitoring under supervision of a multi-disciplinary team of senior consultants comprising of gastroenterologists, hepatologist, critical care and others. His condition is critical but stable.”
Riteish Deshmukh took to Twitter to share this news. He wrote, “I will miss you my friend. #NishikantKamat Rest In Peace."
