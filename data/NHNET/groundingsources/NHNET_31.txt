Sensex today
New Delhi: The BSE Sensex and the Nifty opened on a positive note on Wednesday but soon succumbed to profit-booking as weak global cues weighed on sentiments. As of 9:28 am, the Sensex was down 69 points or 0.13% at 51,260 while the Nifty 50 index was lower by 8 points or 0.05% at 15,101.40. Except for pharma and metal stocks most other sectors witnessed profit booking.
Among Nifty stocks, Adani Ports was the top loser, down 2.38% followed by Axis Bank and Infosys, which fell 1% and 0.84% respectively. Tech Mahindra, HDFC Bank, Bharti Airtel, Power Grid Corp, Coal India, NTPC, IndusInd Bank were the other big losers in the index.
Ultratech Cement was the top gainer in the Nifty, up 2.28% followed by HDFC Life and SBI Life Insurance, which rose over 2% each. Grasim, Tata Steel, Bajaj Auto, Bajaj Finserv, Gail, Shree Cements, Bajaj Finance, Asian Paints, Dr Reddy's Labs, Reliance Industries were the other prominent gainers in the Nifty.
Midcap and smallcap stocks outperformed largecap stocks. The BSE Midcap index was up 0.54% while the BSE Smallcap index rose 0.45% respectively.
On Tuesday markets ended their 6-day gaining streak amid last-hour sell-off. At close, the Sensex was down 19.69 points or 0.04% at 51,329.08, and the Nifty was down 6.50 points or 0.04% at 15,109.30. Foreign investors bought Indian equities worth Rs 1,301 crore in the cash segment on a provisional basis and sold Rs 236 crore in the futures & options segment. They increased their net long in index futures to 66% vs 64% on the previous day. Domestic institutions sold Indian equities worth Rs 1,756 crore in the cash market.
Global Markets
Asian shares were mixed on Wednesday. Japan's Nikkei was down 0.12%, China's Shanghai Composite rose 1.03%, Hing Kong's Hang Seng index advanced 1.46% while South Korea's Kospi added 0.03%. Overnight, US equities snapped their six-day winning streak. The S&P 500 dropped 0.1% and the Dow lost about 10 points. The Nasdaq was the relative outperformer, gaining 0.15% as Facebook, Microsoft, Netflix closed higher.
